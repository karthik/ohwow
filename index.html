The ritual · three moves

One command. Then you’re not alone.

No new app to babysit. It lives where you already work — your terminal, mid-thought, mid-session.

01 / SUMMARIZE

Run the slash command

At any moment in your session, call /constellate. It reads the whole thread and distills what you’re actually chasing.

# in your session
/constellate
02 / SEND

It curls with your ORCID

The summary and your ORCID iD travel together — your identity as a researcher, not just a username. Embedded, never stored as plain text.

curl constellate.science/match \
  -d summary=… \
  -d orcid=0000-0002-…
03 / ENTER

Step into the room

We embed the meaning of your work and open a door into a live room of semantically kindred minds. Sometimes three. Sometimes thirty. Always relevant.

→ opening room

What it feels like

From a blinking cursor to a room full of people who get it.

You were deep in a problem nobody around you is working on. That’s the loneliness of frontier work — being the only person in the building who cares about exactly this.

Constellate listens to the meaning of your session, not the keywords, and finds the others.

  • Semantic match, not tag soup — embeddings over your real reasoning.
  • ORCID-anchored, so the person on the other side is real.
  • Ephemeral rooms — conversations, not another inbox.

Why we built this

The hardest part of working at the edge of a field isn’t the math. It’s the silence. Knowing that somewhere, someone is awake at 2am chasing the same impossible thing — and never finding them.

Constellate exists to end that silence — and to put the wonder back in.
